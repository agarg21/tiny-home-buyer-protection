Movable tiny house as an ADU

Possible path: Movable Tiny House as an ADU under Los Angeles ADU/MTH rules, if the parcel, primary dwelling context, structure, utilities, and permit application satisfy current city requirements.

Official sources

What the official sources say

ADU ordinance context

LADBS says the Los Angeles ADU ordinance defines ADUs, JADUs, and Movable Tiny Houses as ADU types under the local ordinance and state code framework.

LADBS ADU page

MTH permit/design guidance

The LADBS information bulletin is specifically for guidelines on using and permitting a Movable Tiny House as an ADU and defines an MTH as separate independent living quarters for one family subject to listed requirements.

LADBS MTH as ADU bulletin

Housing policy context

Los Angeles City Planning's housing policy page is a supporting source for housing policy context, but parcel-specific zoning still requires city verification.

Before you buy

Verification checklist

Use this as a question list before buying land, ordering a movable tiny house, or making a nonrefundable deposit.

  1. Confirm the parcel is inside the City of Los Angeles, not merely Los Angeles County.
  2. Ask whether the parcel and primary dwelling context can support an ADU/MTH application.
  3. Ask which zoning overlays, hillside/coastal/fire/access constraints, and utility requirements apply.
  4. Ask whether the specific movable tiny house design meets current city MTH requirements.
  5. Check HOA documents, deed restrictions, lender conditions, and insurance requirements.
  6. Save official URLs, staff names, dates, and written answers in a source log.
