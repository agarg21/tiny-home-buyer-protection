Reality check

The seller quote is not the whole project cost

A low structure quote can still become an expensive project if the site is hard to serve, the structure is hard to classify, or financing and insurance do not line up.

Land and placement

Land cost is only one part of placement. Verify zoning, site access, setbacks, utility availability, deed restrictions, and whether the structure type can be used the way you intend.

Utilities and site work

Driveway, grading, drainage, septic, water, and electrical service can change the budget materially. Get written quotes before making a nonrefundable payment.

Permits and inspections

Permit, design, engineering, survey, inspection, and correction costs depend on local rules and the project path. A seller's quote may not include them.

Financing and insurance

Lenders and insurers may treat a THOW, park model, manufactured home, modular home, ADU, or foundation dwelling differently. Confirm eligibility before assuming a monthly payment.

Before you put money down

Buyer-protection checklist

Use this before paying a deposit, buying land, or ordering a home. The goal is to find missing costs while you can still walk away.

  1. Ask the seller what is excluded from the quote: delivery, setup, foundation, utility connections, taxes, permits, and inspections.
  2. Get written site-work quotes for driveway, grading, drainage, electrical service, water, septic or sewer, and any required foundation or anchoring.
  3. Verify the placement path with the planning/building office before relying on the budget.
  4. Ask the lender what property type, certification, title, foundation, appraisal, and insurance conditions are required.
  5. Ask the insurer what structure category and occupancy type they will cover.
  6. Add a contingency, then decide whether the project still makes sense if the expensive unknowns come in high.
