San Diego tiny house guide

Tiny Houses in San Diego: Buy, Build, Stay, or Place One?

Thinking about a tiny house in San Diego? First separate the listing, builder quote, community, or stay from the legal placement question. City sources discuss movable tiny houses through a specific permit path, but county properties, private communities, and specific parcels need separate verification.

Source last checked: 2026-07-08

Can I put a tiny house in San Diego?

Short answer
Maybe. City of San Diego sources discuss movable tiny houses in an ADU / companion-unit-related path, but only when the parcel, unit, utilities, and permits fit current city requirements.
Why ADU matters
For this city, ADU-related rules are where the official movable tiny house path appears. That does not make every tiny home, THOW, RV, kit, park model, community, or stay eligible for long-term placement.
What is not proof
A listing, rental, community page, THOW, park model, RV, or builder quote does not prove parcel eligibility, unit compliance, long-term occupancy, or permit approval.
First action
Confirm the parcel is inside the City of San Diego, classify the unit, then ask the seller, builder, community, or host which rules and exclusions they assume.
Confidence and scope
Official-source confidence is high for an official San Diego MTH / ADU-related path. Parcel eligibility is unverified. This is city-specific guidance.
If the parcel is outside the City of San Diego, stop here.

This page does not answer rules for San Diego County, another city, an unincorporated area, a private community, an HOA, a lender, or an insurer.

First checks

Do these three things before you call a seller, builder, or community

A tiny-home price, community page, or builder quote is only useful after you know the jurisdiction, unit type, and use they assume.

1. Confirm the parcel city

Use the parcel address, not the metro area or listing location. The exact city controls which office and rules apply.

2. Classify the unit or stay

Ask whether it is a movable tiny house, ADU, THOW, park model, RV, shell, kit, long-term community space, or short stay.

3. Ask what the sale price, quote, or stay includes

For a listed tiny home, builder quote, community option, or stay, get written assumptions for permits, rental duration, utilities, delivery, setup, inspections, lot/lease terms, warranty, financing, insurance, and exclusions.

Likely worth investigating if

  • The parcel is inside the City of San Diego.
  • You are comparing a movable tiny house, ADU-related path, or builder quote with a specific site in mind.
  • The seller or builder can explain classification, permit path, delivery/setup, and utilities in writing.

Not ready to pay if

  • You are mixing short-stay/community research with ownership or placement.
  • The seller cannot explain whether the unit is an MTH, THOW, park model, RV, ADU, shell, or kit.
  • The price leaves out site work, utilities, delivery/setup, lease terms, permit corrections, or inspection responsibility.

Official sources

What the official sources say

Movable tiny house bulletin

Information Bulletin 403 is the city's movable tiny house-specific official bulletin and should be used for implementation details, with current code verified before publishing a high-specificity claim.

City of San Diego Information Bulletin 403

ADU and JADU code section

San Diego Municipal Code Section 141.0302 governs ADUs and JADUs and contains restrictions on rental term, lot/zone context, size, setbacks, coastal overlay, fire, parking, owner occupancy, and related requirements.

San Diego Municipal Code Chapter 14, Article 1, Division 3

Adopted code update context

City of San Diego official materials describe movable tiny houses as adopted into companion unit / junior unit regulations and as falling under the state's ADU definition.

Builder distinction

Tiny-home builder vs ADU builder

San Diego builder searches can point to tiny-home sellers, ADU contractors, backyard-unit vendors, trailer/shell providers, or general contractors. Those are not interchangeable.

  • Ask whether the quote is for a conventional ADU, movable tiny house, THOW, park model, RV, shell, kit, or another category.
  • Ask who handles plans, permits, inspections, delivery, anchoring, utility connections, title/registration questions, and site work.
  • Ask what is excluded from the price: engineering, foundation/pad, utility trenching, upgrades, crane/set work, taxes, permits, and corrections.
  • Ask whether their claim is specific to the City of San Diego, San Diego County, or California generally.

Community and stay results

A tiny-house stay is not the same as permission to place one

Some San Diego results are vacation stays, communities, groups, or examples of tiny-house living. They may help you explore the lifestyle, but they do not answer whether your parcel, unit, lease, utilities, or long-term occupancy plan is allowed.

  • Ask whether the option is a short stay, long-term rental, lot lease, owned unit, or community membership.
  • Ask the exact jurisdiction and whether the result is inside the City of San Diego.
  • Ask about allowed duration, utilities, parking, insurance, guest/tenant rules, and written lease or lot terms.

Parcel-specific checks

What the city still has to confirm

These are the details a page, seller, builder, community, or host cannot confirm for your parcel or long-term plan. Ask City of San Diego Development Services or Planning before relying on a listing, quote, lease, or deposit deadline.

  • Whether a specific parcel is eligible.
  • Whether the proposed structure meets the current movable tiny house standards.
  • How coastal overlay, fire hazard severity, beach impact parking, or other overlays affect a specific site.
  • Whether HOA, deed, lender, or insurance constraints apply.
  • Whether septic, utility hookups, fire access, parking, rental, or permit details block the plan.

Before you pay

Verification checklist

Use this before buying land, ordering a movable tiny house, hiring a builder, booking a long-term community option, or making a nonrefundable deposit.

  1. Confirm the exact parcel city and save the source you used.
  2. Ask whether the parcel, zone, and existing/proposed dwelling context fit the current ADU / companion unit-related framework.
  3. Classify the unit: movable tiny house, conventional ADU, THOW, park model, RV, shell, kit, or another category.
  4. Ask how coastal overlay, fire hazard severity, beach impact parking, access, and utility requirements affect the site.
  5. Ask whether the specific movable tiny house design meets current city standards.
  6. Ask the seller or builder for written exclusions: permits, plans, engineering, site work, utility hookups, delivery, setup, warranty, taxes, financing, and insurance.
  7. Confirm rental-term, parking, owner-occupancy, address, utility, lender, and insurer constraints.
  8. Save official URLs, staff names, dates, and written answers in a source log.
