## Interpretation

- If comparing singular terms only, `tiny house` is more popular than `tiny home`.
- If comparing exact head terms overall, `tiny homes` is the largest of the four.
- The `tiny house` keyword family appears larger in Semrush keyword variations: 117.3K variations and 3.1M total variation volume.
- The `tiny home` keyword family appears smaller but more commercially useful for some terms: 65.1K variations and 1.8M total variation volume.

## Strategy

Use both terms.

- Brand: `Tiny Home Clarity`.
- Product/page titles: use the query language when the demand is clear, such as `Where Can I Put a Tiny House?`.
- Body copy: naturally include both `tiny house` and `tiny home`, especially when explaining type distinctions.
- Navigation/category language: prefer `tiny home` where it sounds broader and more product-like.
- SEO targeting: do not treat the terms as interchangeable for all pages. Choose the dominant term per query cluster.

## Practical Rule

For the first tool, keep:

```text
Where Can I Put a Tiny House?
```

That phrasing matches the existing target query. Use supporting copy to say the tool helps with tiny homes, THOWs, park models, ADUs, RVs, modular/manufactured homes, and small cabins.
