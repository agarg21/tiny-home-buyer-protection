# Content Principles

Last updated: 2026-07-07

## Voice

Be skeptical, practical, buyer-protective, source-backed, and clear about uncertainty.

Use plain language. Explain tradeoffs. Say when the answer depends on local verification.

## What To Publish

Prefer:

- Tools.
- Calculators.
- Checklists.
- Comparison tables.
- Templates.
- Source-backed verification guides.
- Original local datasets with official source links.
- Product specs that can become useful interactive pages.

Avoid:

- Dreamy lifestyle content.
- Generic inspiration posts.
- Definitive legal answers.
- Mass city/state pages.
- Thin affiliate listicles.
- Claims that tiny homes are always cheap.
- Scaled AI content or mass-generated low-value article batches.

## Claim Handling

When discussing zoning, placement, building codes, health rules, financing, insurance, or safety:

- Use official sources whenever possible.
- Separate measured data, tool estimates, assumptions, and opinions.
- Include last checked dates for changeable facts.
- Mark uncertain or unavailable data as `UNKNOWN`.
- Use confidence levels for local records.
- Make verification steps explicit.

Preferred wording:

- "Possible path."
- "This needs local verification."
- "Ask the local planning/building department."
- "This page cannot determine HOA, deed restriction, lender, or insurance constraints."

Avoid:

- "This is legal."
- "You can put a tiny home here."
- "Guaranteed approval."
- "Always cheaper."

## Index-Worthiness Standard

A page deserves to be indexed only if it helps a reader make or verify a decision. It should have at least one of:

- Interactive tool or calculator.
- Original table or dataset.
- Official-source synthesis.
- Strong checklist or template.
- Useful comparison framework.
- Specific, source-backed caveats.
