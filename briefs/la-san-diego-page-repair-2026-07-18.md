# Brief: Repair Los Angeles And San Diego Tiny-House Pages

Date: 2026-07-18

## Objective

Turn the existing Los Angeles and San Diego source-backed pages into useful
answer-first local guides for mixed tiny-house intent.

The pages should no longer read mainly as cautious source memos. They should
help a broad, uncertain searcher understand what "tiny house in this city" can
mean, which options are probably relevant, what official sources support, and
what to verify before spending money.

## Search Intent To Serve

### San Diego

The broad query `tiny house san diego` has mixed local/commercial intent:

- listings / tiny homes for sale,
- builders,
- communities,
- rentals/stays,
- THOW and placement feasibility,
- some official/news/rules-adjacent intent.

The page should not pretend the query is only about zoning.

### Los Angeles

The broad query `tiny house los angeles` and sale query variants are dominated
by listings, builders, ADU/backyard-home vendors, nonprofit/shelter village
results, Reddit/personal experience, and broad commercial pages.

The official MTH-as-ADU path is useful, but exact `tiny house adu los angeles`
demand is very low. The page needs to connect ADU/MTH source facts to buyer
shopping/building decisions.

ADU-builder demand is adjacent, not the same as tiny-home build demand. `ADU
builders los angeles` and `ADU builders san diego` each showed about 480 monthly
searches with high CPC in the 2026-07-18 pull, but many of those searchers want
conventional ADU contractors. Use those terms only to explain the difference
between a conventional ADU, a movable tiny house as ADU where supported, and a
tiny home that may not have a legal placement path.

## Required Page Structure

Each page should lead with a compact answer section:

1. Plain-English answer to what the city page can and cannot tell the reader.
2. The main meanings of the broad query in this city:
   - buy or browse a tiny home,
   - build/order one,
   - place one on a parcel,
   - use one as an ADU/backyard unit where supported,
   - find a community, village, rental, or short stay.
3. Which of those meanings Tiny Home Clarity can help with, and which are
   adjacent or out of scope.
4. What the official source supports.
5. Who the source-backed path may fit.
6. Stop if.
7. Next office/question.
8. What remains unverified.

Do not make the first screen a generic "choose your intent" menu. Broad-query
searchers may not know the right vocabulary yet. The page should translate the
local SERP landscape into practical choices and then guide the reader toward the
official-source placement checks.

## San Diego Page Work Items

- Retitle the page so it can serve broad mixed intent, not only ADU/MTH:
  candidate H1: `Tiny Houses in San Diego: Buy, Build, Stay, or Place One?`
- Keep the current City of San Diego MTH/ADU-related source section, but make it
  one path inside a broader decision page.
- Add an above-the-fold "what this search usually means" section that explains
  the difference between listings, builders, communities/stays, and legal
  placement. Do not require the reader to self-select before they understand the
  options.
- Add a "City vs County" note. Do not assume City of San Diego rules apply to
  San Diego County or unincorporated areas.
- Add a buyer-protective checklist before contacting builders/sellers:
  classification, placement path, delivery/setup, utilities, permits,
  warranty, financing, insurance, and written city/county answers.
- Add a concise "tiny-home builder vs ADU builder" distinction. Do not imply an
  ADU contractor can legally place every tiny home, and do not imply a
  tiny-home builder can solve city/county placement.
- Add a "not a fit if" list:
  outside city scope, no parcel confirmation, seller cannot explain
  classification/path, community/stay intent confused with ownership.

## Los Angeles Page Work Items

- Retitle the page so it can serve broad mixed intent:
  candidate H1: `Tiny Houses in Los Angeles: Buy, Build, or Use as an ADU?`
- Keep the City of Los Angeles movable tiny house as ADU source section, but
  make it one decision path.
- Add an above-the-fold "what this search usually means" section that explains
  the difference between sale listings, backyard-home/ADU vendors,
  nonprofit/shelter village results, communities/stays, and legal placement.
  Do not require the reader to self-select before they understand the options.
- Add a "City of Los Angeles vs Los Angeles County" scope warning.
- Add a buyer-protective checklist before contacting backyard-home/ADU vendors:
  parcel location, ADU eligibility, MTH design requirements, utility scope,
  delivery/setup, permits, financing, insurance, and written city answers.
- Add a concise "movable tiny house as ADU vs conventional ADU" distinction,
  because LA has an official MTH-as-ADU path but ADU-builder searches are not
  automatically tiny-home searches.
- Add a "not a fit if" list:
  outside city scope, looking for countywide permission, seller uses ADU/MTH
  language without permit/source details, or the buyer has not checked deed/HOA
  constraints.

## Shared Constraints

- Do not claim parcel approval or legal permission.
- Do not imply all tiny homes are cheap.
- Keep official source URLs visible.
- Keep source last checked dates.
- Make the page more decisive about official facts and next steps.
- Use `UNKNOWN` where metrics or source coverage are unavailable.
- Do not add lead capture, analytics, affiliate links, or partner/vendor
  recommendations.

## Success Criteria

- A broad-query reader can understand the local tiny-house landscape within the
  first screen, even if they did not know the right terms before searching.
- The page says what the official source supports before listing caveats.
- The page distinguishes shopping/stay/community intent from legal placement
  intent.
- The page distinguishes tiny-home build intent from ADU contractor intent.
- The page gives practical stop/next-step guidance without legal overclaims.
- SEO Research & Review can review the rendered page against this brief before
  release.
