# Brief: Tiny-Home Build Decision And Builder Due Diligence

Date: 2026-07-20

Action: `THC-BUILD-001`

## Objective

Scope a national, buyer-protective asset for people deciding whether to build a
tiny home, buy plans, buy a kit, buy a shell, use a trailer, or hire a tiny-home
builder.

This brief is internal planning only. It does not authorize publishing a page,
creating a builder directory, recommending vendors, making legal conclusions,
or starting implementation.

## Demand Read

The 2026-07-18 DataForSEO pull shows meaningful national build-stage demand,
with close-variant grouping caveats:

- Broad build / builder / contractor cluster: about 40,500 monthly searches.
- Plans / floor plans: about 27,100 monthly searches.
- Kits: about 12,100 monthly searches.
- Trailer / shell: about 9,900 / 3,600 monthly searches.
- Builders near me: about 4,400 monthly searches.
- Building codes: about 1,600 monthly searches.
- THOW build: about 390 monthly searches.

Do not add these rows together as a traffic forecast. Google Ads groups close
variants, and many searchers overlap across build, plans, kits, shells,
builders, and cost research.

Local exact tiny-house builder demand is much smaller:

- Los Angeles tiny-house builders: about 50 monthly searches.
- San Diego tiny-house builders: about 90 monthly searches.
- California tiny-house builders: about 720 monthly searches.

ADU-builder demand is adjacent, not the same market:

- `adu builders los angeles`: about 480 monthly searches.
- `adu builders san diego`: about 480 monthly searches.

Those ADU searches may involve conventional ADU contractors rather than tiny
homes. The future asset should explain the distinction, not target ADU-builder
city pages.

## Target Searcher

Primary reader: beginner to intermediate tiny-home researcher.

Likely situations:

- Saw tiny-house plans, kits, shells, or trailers and wants to know which path
  is realistic.
- Wants to hire a builder but does not know what a builder quote excludes.
- Is comparing THOW, park model, RV, modular, site-built ADU, kit, shell, or
  custom tiny home language.
- Wants to know what to verify before paying for plans, a deposit, a trailer,
  delivery, or a builder contract.

Advanced reader needs:

- Clear source and document requests.
- Risk questions for permits, code, utilities, transport, insurance, financing,
  warranty, and exclusions.
- Boundaries on what Tiny Home Clarity cannot determine nationally.

## Recommended Asset

Working title:

`Should You Build, Buy a Kit, Buy a Shell, or Hire a Tiny-Home Builder?`

Working format:

- Decision guide first.
- Comparison table second.
- Due-diligence checklist third.
- Optional future interactive tool only after the guide structure is reviewed.

Primary job-to-be-done:

Help a reader avoid choosing the wrong tiny-home build path before spending
money.

The asset should help readers compare:

- DIY build from plans.
- Tiny-house kit.
- Shell.
- Trailer / THOW build.
- Custom tiny-home builder.
- Conventional ADU / backyard unit contractor.
- Park model / RV-style product.

## Page Strategy

The first screen should answer:

- "Which build path am I actually considering?"
- "What can go wrong before I pay?"
- "What should I verify before choosing plans, a kit, shell, trailer, builder,
  or ADU contractor?"

The page should not open as a generic SEO article about tiny-house building. It
should act like a buyer-protection decision aid.

Recommended sections:

1. Short answer: choose the path by risk, not just advertised price.
2. Decision table: plans vs kit vs shell vs trailer/THOW vs builder vs ADU
   contractor.
3. What each quote or product usually does not prove.
4. Questions to ask before paying.
5. Build-path red flags.
6. What needs local verification.
7. Documents to request.
8. When to stop and get a local answer first.
9. Next step: use the existing placement and cost tools before paying a deposit.

## Required Comparison Table

Rows should include:

## Required Due-Diligence Questions

The asset should ask readers to collect written answers for:

- Structure classification.
- Intended use and occupancy.
- Placement jurisdiction.
- Permit path.
- Building code or construction standard.
- Trailer, foundation, anchoring, or setup assumptions.
- Delivery and access constraints.
- Utility connections.
- Septic/sewer and water assumptions.
- Heating/cooling and insulation assumptions.
- Financing assumptions.
- Insurance assumptions.
- Warranty coverage and exclusions.
- Change-order and correction responsibility.
- Refund, cancellation, and deposit terms.

## Claim Boundaries

Do not claim:

- A tiny home can legally be placed on a specific property.
- One build path is always cheaper.
- A listed price is a complete move-in cost.
- A builder, kit seller, or ADU contractor is vetted or recommended.
- ADU-builder demand is the same as tiny-home-builder demand.
- A national page can answer local zoning, code, HOA, lender, or insurer
  constraints.

Use:

- "May fit."
- "Ask for in writing."
- "Verify with the local office."
- "This does not prove placement, permit approval, financing, or insurance."
- `UNKNOWN` for unavailable current CPC, ranking difficulty, traffic,
  backlinks, or conversion estimates.
